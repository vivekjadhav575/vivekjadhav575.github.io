BAJRA

UREA : It is a nitrogenous fertilizer that promotes vegetative growth and helps in the development of healthy leaves and stems.
DI-AMMONIUM PHOSPHATE (DAP) : This compound fertilizer provides both nitrogen and phosphorus, supporting root development and overall plant vigor.
POTASSIUM CHLORIDE (Muriate of Potash) : It supplies potassium, which is essential for improving drought tolerance, enhancing grain quality and fruiting in bajra plants.
SINGLE SUPER PHOSPHAtTE (SSP) : It is a phosphorus fertilizer that aids in root establishment and early growth stages of bajra plants.
COMPLEX FERTILIZERS : Bajra crops can benefit from balanced nutrition provided by complex fertilizers such as NPK 20:20:0 or 10:26:26. These fertilizers contain varying ratios of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, ensuring optimal growth and development.

COFFEE

NITROGENOUS FERTILIZERS : Urea and ammonium sulfate are often used to supply nitrogen, which promotes vine growth and leaf development.
PHOSPHORUS FERTILIZERS : Di-ammonium phosphate (DAP) and rock phosphate are used to provide phosphorus, which supports root development and enhances flowering and fruiting in grapevines.
POTASSIUM FERTILIZERS : Muriate of potash (Potassium chloride) or potassium sulfate are applied to supply potassium, an essential nutrient for overall plant health, fruit quality and disease resistance.
MICRONUTRIENT FERTILIZERS : Grapevines may require micronutrients like zinc, boron, iron, and manganese. These can be supplied through specific fertilizers formulated with the required micronutrients.
ORGANIC FERTILIZERS : Organic sources such as compost, farmyard manure, and organic biofertilizers can be used to improve soil fertility, enhance nutrient availability, and promote long-term soil health in grape vineyards.

COTTON

NITROGENOUS FERTILIZERS: Cotton plants require a significant amount of nitrogen for vegetative growth, flower formation, and fiber development. Urea is a commonly used nitrogen fertilizer to supply the required nitrogen to cotton plants.
PHOSPHORUS FERTILIZERS: Phosphorus promotes root development, early growth, and flowering in cotton plants. Di-ammonium phosphate (DAP) or rock phosphate can be used as phosphorus fertilizers for cotton cultivation.
POTASSIUM FERTILIZERS: Potassium supports overall plant health, disease resistance, and improves cotton fiber quality. Potassium chloride (Muriate of Potash) or potassium sulfate are commonly used sources of potassium fertilizer.

MAIZE(KHARIF)

UREA : Urea is a nitrogenous fertilizer that promotes vegetative growth, leaf formation, and overall plant development in maize.
DI-AMMONIUM PHOSPHATE (DAP) : DAP is a compound fertilizer that supplies both nitrogen and phosphorus to support root development, flowering, and grain formation in maize plants. POTASSIUM CHLORIDE (Muriate of Potash) : Maize crops benefit from potassium for improving stalk strength, disease resistance, and enhancing grain quality. Muriate of potash is a common source of potassium fertilizer.
COMPLEX FERTILIZERS : Balanced complex fertilizers with varying ratios of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K) like NPK 20:20:0 or 10:26:26 are often used to provide comprehensive nutrition for optimal maize growth and yield.
ZINC SULPHATE : Zinc is an important micronutrient for maize. Zinc sulphate is applied as a foliar spray or soil amendment to address zinc deficiencies in the soil and promote healthy maize growth.
